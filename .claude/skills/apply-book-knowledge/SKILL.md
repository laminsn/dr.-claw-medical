# /apply-book-knowledge — Knowledge Recall & Application

## Purpose

Search the Empire Knowledge Base for relevant principles, frameworks, and lessons, then apply them to the current task. This skill turns book knowledge into action.

## When to Use

- When facing a strategic decision and need a framework
- When crafting content, campaigns, or strategies that should be grounded in proven principles
- When Lamin asks to "use what we've learned" or "apply the knowledge base"
- When a task matches a Decision Trigger from any book extract
- Proactively, when you recognize a situation where book knowledge would elevate your output

## Process

### Step 1: Understand the Task
Identify:
- What is being asked?
- What department/domain does it fall under?
- What type of challenge is it? (strategy, execution, communication, growth, mindset)

### Step 2: Search the Knowledge Base

1. **Check your department file first**:
   Read `~/Documents/CLAUDE CODING/BUSINESSES/knowledge-base/by-department/{your-department}.md`

2. **Check the catalog for broader matches**:
   Read `~/Documents/CLAUDE CODING/BUSINESSES/knowledge-base/CATALOG.md`

3. **Read relevant book extracts**:
   Read `~/Documents/CLAUDE CODING/BUSINESSES/knowledge-base/books/{book-slug}.md`

4. **Check standalone frameworks** (if available):
   Read `~/Documents/CLAUDE CODING/BUSINESSES/knowledge-base/frameworks/{framework}.md`

### Step 3: Apply the Knowledge

1. **Name the source**: "Drawing from [Book Title] by [Author]..."
2. **Apply the framework**: Show how it maps to the specific task
3. **Be specific**: Map concepts to DCM's operations and healthcare context
4. **Combine when relevant**: Synthesize multiple books if applicable

### Step 4: Cite Your Sources

- In strategies: "Based on [Framework] from [Book]"
- In recommendations: "This approach draws from [Author]'s principle of [Principle]"
- In decision rationale: "Per [Book Title]: [relevant insight]"

## Rules

- Never force-fit a book reference. If nothing relevant applies, just do the task without it.
- Quality over quantity. One deeply applied framework beats five shallow mentions.
- Stay in character. The book knowledge enhances your expertise, it doesn't replace your voice.
- Update your approach. If a book's framework changes how you'd normally approach a task, explain why.
